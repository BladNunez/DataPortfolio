Project Overview

I wanted to see how a retail store can perform better to keep customers and to expand their domain in the sales world by reaching new customers as well as keeping costs low. The dataset I used was from a fake retail store based in the United Kingdom but shipped worldwide in the years of 2010 - 2012. It contains what product they sold and where the customer is located country wise as well as quantity and price of the product the customer bought and the date they bought it on. I found insights and gave recommendations for this retail company down below.

Insights/Recommendations

  • From the many items sold we should get rid of all products that did not sell at least 100 items. From analysis this would bring the initial number of 3556 items in our store down to 537. We can maintain the store efficiently as well as lower costs on storage and buying new products.

  • We should focus more marketing in western/eastern Europe. Most of our customers are from there so we should build a higher base of customers there and after market in other regions of the world.

  • Prioritize the top 10 products that sold by most quantity and also prioritize the top 5 products sold by unit price. By doing this it guarantees products selling meaning revenue.

  • With the last three months in 2012 having a high purchase frequency I would recommend to sell the same products from those months the next year.

Data Cleaning/Preprocessing

Check for missing values, Dropped Unused columns, Split column into new ones, Initial 'invoiceDate' column had years just with 1 digit so removed those and a null date for data consistency

Feature Engineering

To show that orders were cancelled or fufilled I created an 'orderStatus' column that contained a text to show if the order was fufilled or cancelled. I knew if they were cancelled if the invoice number column was a zero and the quantity was a negative number. I split the initial 'invoiceDate' column into two columns: 'PurchaseDate' , 'PurchaseHour'. This showed the date of when the order was fufilled and the hour. This was for better readability.

Data Analysis

`select month(PurchaseDate) as Month, year(PurchaseDate) as year,count(CustomerID) as No_Purchase_Customers from retail where orderStatus = "Cancelled" and month(PurchaseDate) in (10,11,12) group by month(PurchaseDate), year(PurchaseDate);`
