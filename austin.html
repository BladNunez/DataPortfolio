Project Overview

In this project I analyze bikeshare trips in the city of Austin,TX from 2013 - 2024. In the year 2024 our data is only updated up until February. Our project contains information about bikeshare trips in the city such as the duration of the trip in minutes, the starting and ending stations a bike was used from and placed back in, the location of those bikes and the date and time when the bikes were used. Our project goals are listed down below:

1.) Analyze bike trip durations to understand average trip lengths and peak usage times.
2.) Identify popular start and end stations to optimize bike distribution and station maintenance.
3.) Explore subscriber types to understand user demographics and preferences.
4.) Visualize bike usage trends over time to identify seasonal variations.

Insights/Recommendations

  • 2018 has the most volume of trips with almost 400,000 trips for the year

  • August - November of 2023 contained the most volume of trips

  • The most popular hours of bike trips in 2023 was from 1Pm - 6Pm with 5Pm having the most volume

  • 21st speedway pcl, dean keeton speedway, ziller park and others are the most popular start/end stations so we need to maintain and update these stations due to the heavy volume it receives

  • Majority of riders throughout the years ride for 2 - 100 minutes

  • I recommend to drop the ride scout single ride subscription due to not having much usage and utilize specials for more bike use

Data Sources

I used the Austin bikeshare dataset from Google BigQuery. I was able to connect to Google BigQuery using a client object from the google.cloud library and running a query in python to be able to load the dataset into a pandas dataframe to begin analysis.
